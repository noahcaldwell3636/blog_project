Blue Collar Approach

Along with washing cars and landscaping, I was a mechanic's assistant for heavy machinery and tractor trailers. Freeze or melt, our division had to keep ~100 vehicles across ~15 locations operational. This meant 50-65 hour weeks crawling in oiled coated engine guts. I cherish the respect I gained there for blue collar workers. It was during these months that I would come home to shower, eat, and take online python courses before bed - to one day create elegant software.

Python is my preference for high-level programming. I have experience with Java, JavaScript, and C++. For the past two years, I have been working with the Django Web Framework. My workflow consists of using Figma for design and Webflow to translate the design to HTML, CSS, JavaScript. I then move the static files to Django for custom functionalities.

Fuller Stack

Data scientist

Large datasets, dirty data, and recording errors were topics I've spent many hours navigating while at Yard Works LLC. I used a combination of python and excel, pulling queries using an ODBC. I was then able to clean and provide insights from queries as large as ~15 million data points. Jim Bollinger, our chief executive, told me I had accomplished what 3rd party contractors have not in the past two years.

I love playing golf, bow hunting, and exploring other's passions. I live a principled life so I may maneuver life's obstacles in accordance with my values. Systems' thinking is my ideal framework.

Personal Bio

               upbringing    

Before I settled on software development, I was interested in being a working man. As a son would, I wanted to be like my father. Working hard in the office, traveling,

building, renovating, and being the man of the house. He taught me

the enjoyment of a project well done.
‍
        As soon as I was able, I'd wash cars after school and landscape   on the weekends. Both would prove difficult at times, but quality           work was rewarded - which is better than the alternative
        of most hourly wages my buddies were working.

       I could not complain when I was in high school. I managed           to have an awesome group of friends, a spot on a competitive            lacrosse team, and was voted class treasurer for     
    three years in a row. Virginia Tech lay down the road.

        Higher education...

There in Blacksburg, Virginia I would learn more about myself than I ever had, in a short time period. I was spoiled with great

dining and as many young bright people that a man could ever talk to. I felt lucky to be there.

All great things can be great curse if misused. Although I managed an impressive academic year, as the semesters went be I was wearing myself

thin. In highschool I was never challenged enough to be required sacrifice for school. Try to do everything - never do one thing well.
It's was like driving a car that's out
of fuel then racing the
Daytona 500.

    .revelations.

A radical change to my behavior was
in order. I feared falling into mediocrity.
Big changes were necessary so I left school
and found a mechanic's position at Yard Works, a
land material cultivator, manufacturer, wholesaler,
and retailer. Unfortunately my college environment
hosted too many temptations to change for the better.
I hoped for a more holistic sense of accomplishment.

Back to work.                 

Since I was a novice mechanic at best, I was given tasks that surrounded the division. I knew I was making the correct choice when the lead of the division, Alan Cyberman, immediately left me with a project to renovate a shipping container. This turned into me reorganizing their entire air, fuel, and oil filters in that container. This eventually turned into me photographing, indexing, labeling, and inputting information for the entire company's fleet across sites across Virginia. I enjoyed being given a goal with liberty to find my own solutions.

Those ten hour work days, two hours of commute, and strenuous labor left me with about one hour to myself after eating and scrubbing oil out of my fingernails. I spent that time taking online programming courses. With no distractions there were few hours wasted.

       ..horizons..         

After I taking these online courses - I made my first custom application: a blackjack game built with the python library Tkinter. Although I would never brag about the app, I love each line of spaghetti code that created that program. It signified to me that I was capable of creation.
‍
The Django web framework seemed like a good next step. I wanted to find real use cases for my programs. So I made a website built on top of Yard Work's ERP system. It was an equipment management website and the beginnings of an automated reporting system. A modest project I started in my little free time solved a problem
the company had been contracting out solutions to for
the past 3 years, to little progress. The mechanics
would have a simple way to browse all the
vehicles, their past repairs, and maintenance
schedule. The Reporting system was the
beginning of myself trying to automate
the reports I was doing for the
executives. These reports would
involve me parsing over hundreds of
thousands of rows invoice data in a
combination of python and excel.

After presenting the website and
revamping their equipment
management module in their ERP
system. I was promoted to the
first internal IT specialist in the
company. I was given benefits,
salary, and half-paid tuition months
after starting as a maintenance
mechanic. This time was a critical
period where I went from being
unsure of myself- to creating a new IT 
position comprised of keeping the company
online, preparing and consulting our cloud
integration, automating tasks, and building business tools.

          today

After this I started working on typical blogging and personal pages. I also created a Flood Forecaster that pulls government data from NOAA. I read the XML data into a Plotly-Dash application that allows for live metrics and graphing.

The last three months I have been using Figma and Webflow to help some friends with a cryptocurrency project. Although aspects outside of the website never came to fruition, I am thankful for that time to learn a game-changing frontend tool like Webflow. Oh the time i've spent tinkering with html and css...

I now find myself looking for work so I can pay for the last 20 credits of my college career, debt-free. Contact me if you think I can benefit your vision and build amazing software in the process. I specialize in backend-web development and enjoy working on projects involving economics, finance, and cryptocurrencies.
